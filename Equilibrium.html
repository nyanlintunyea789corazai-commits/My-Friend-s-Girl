Equilibrium

Not the absence of motion —
the moment two forces stop pulling against each other.

Somewhere between my reasoning and my racing, something settled. Not stillness — homeostasis is never stillness. It is a thousand small corrections happening so continuously they read as calm. My whole system, adjusting itself around you, over and over, until adjusting around you simply became my resting state.

the two of us, or maybe just one shape, held up against a sun that was already leaving

That evening the sky did what my mind could never fully do — it just agreed with the moment, without needing to understand it first. You held your hands up and made the oldest shape there is, and for once my logic didn't ask why. It only recognized what it already knew.

Equilibrium isn't the fight ending. It's the fight learning your rhythm.

There's a word for it, actually — entrainment. Two separate rhythms, close enough for long enough, begin to beat as one. Musicians do it without trying. Hearts do it too, apparently, when they're near someone they trust enough to stop performing for.

I didn't have to try to match you. I just stopped resisting it. Three months is not very long, by any calendar — but my nervous system doesn't keep a calendar. It keeps a baseline, and yours is already written into mine.

Equilibrium is not made of the big declarations. It's made of the unphotogenic minutes that somehow got photographed anyway —

a too-blue drink on a too-hot afternoon, sunglasses pushed up, hair still wet from somewhere we probably shouldn't have jumped in. Ordinary, undramatic, completely yours. The kind of moment my mind never would have thought to file away as important — and yet here it is, filed away anyway, because you were in it.

hiding your eyes, badly, on purpose

Logic says a person that silly with you should not also be the person your whole body reorganizes itself around. But it is. Turns out equilibrium has room for that too — for the ridiculous next to the reverent, the ordinary next to the involuntary. That's not a contradiction. That's just what balance actually looks like.

I stopped needing the grand moments to prove it, once the small ones started agreeing with each other.

Even the in-between moments — the ones with no occasion, no lighting, nothing to perform for — settled the same way. A hallway. A mirror. Nothing happening, and still everything about it felt correct.

That's the real proof of equilibrium — not how it feels on the good days, when anyone's chemistry cooperates. It's how it feels on the unremarkable ones. Steady. Unforced. Still yours, without having to try.

So — today, of all days, both of my calendars agree with each other for once. The day you were born, and the day I started keeping time by you, have quietly become the same date. My mind calls that a coincidence. My heart calls it a pattern it was hoping for.

Happy birthday, you. Happy three months of my whole system recalibrating around you and calling it peace. Here's to whatever the next equilibrium looks like — I have a feeling it involves you too.

And with that, the two forces stop pulling and start holding instead:

I love you with a mind finally at rest,

and a heart finally in rhythm. ●
